About silk moths

Silk moths (lepidopteran) are a large species of moths who are native to Asia, however can be found in almost all places in the world. for thousands of years the catipillars of these majestic insects have been used to make silk. some species such as the domestic silk moth (Bombyx mori) have been domesticated in order to create a more reliable production of silk.

The journey through life

Many silk moths live a rather brief life living only two to five days. Many silk moths have evolved to mate and die or be part of the circle of life contributing to the food chain. In a silk moths short life the males preform a flutter dance in order to attract a female. after the female lays her eggs it takes around two weeks or fourteen days for the eggs to hatch in to silkworms. If the silkworm is able to survive they wrap themselfs into their silk caccon where the cycle begins again.

Types of silk moths

While it is impossible to name every silk moth in the world with over 2,300 species here is a list of some of our favorites

  • Attacus atlas, the Atlas moth
  • Actias luna, The luna moth
  • Argema mittrei, the comet moth
  • Hyalophora cecropia, the cecropia moth
  • Eupackardia calleta, the calleta silkmoth
